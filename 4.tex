% The iterative design study (Sim~1--Sim~6) converges on a single recommended configuration: 
% \textbf{Pd--Ag alloy thick-film paste, 20~traces, Chebyshev spacing with $p = 1.3$, driven at the nominal battery voltage $V_\text{in} = \SI{12}{\volt}$.} This section summarises the key performance metrics leading to that design, the gain obtained from trace-pattern optimisation, and the robustness of the final configuration across real-world weather conditions.

\subsubsection{Trace Pattern Optimisation}

With material, trace count, and voltage fixed at the baseline optimum (Pd--Ag alloy, 20~traces, \SI{12}{\volt}), the trace pattern was optimised without adding any cost. Two strategies were compared: a zoned layout (Sim~4, 6/8/6 distribution) and a continuous Chebyshev distribution with variable exponent $p$ (Sim~5). 
The zoned layout showed only a small improvement time (\SI{11}{\minute} defrost, $T_\text{min,SS} = \SI{289.3}{\kelvin}$). The Chebyshev approach, which changes the trace pattern for different values of $p$, proved more effective: sweeping $p$ identified an optimum at $p = 1.3$ and \Cref{tab:results-pattern} compares all four layouts.

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \caption{Trace-pattern comparison (Pd--Ag alloy, 20 traces, $V_\text{in} = \SI{12}{\volt}$).}
    \label{tab:results-pattern}
    \begin{tabular}{@{}lccc@{}}
        \toprule
        \textbf{Layout} & \textbf{Defrost time} & $T_\text{min,SS}$ \textbf{(K)} & \textbf{Gain vs.\ uniform} \\
        \midrule
        Uniform (baseline)           & 12\,min & 288.2 & --- \\
        Zoned 6/8/6                  & 11\,min & 289.3 & $-$8\%, $+$\SI{1.1}{\kelvin} \\
        Chebyshev $p = 1.0$          & 8\,min  & 292.6 & $-$33\%, $+$\SI{4.4}{\kelvin} \\
        \textbf{Chebyshev $p = 1.3$} & \textbf{6\,min}  & \textbf{295.0} & $-$\textbf{50\%}, $+$\textbf{\SI{6.8}{\kelvin}} \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The Chebyshev $p = 1.3$ spacing halves the defrost time and raises the steady-state minimum temperature by \SI{6.8}{\kelvin} relative to the base configuration,
 purely through geometric redistribution of the same 20~traces. The design also remains within the SAE~J216 visibility limit with \SI{4.25}{\percent} obscuration.
Combining the material, electrical, and geometric optima gives the final design for the trace pattern. Sim~6 further confirmed that this configuration is robust 
 five weather scenarios ranging from \SIrange{-20}{10}{\celsius} ambient and \SIrange{10}{40}{\watt\per\metre\squared\per\kelvin} 
 for external convection. Every case reaches dew temperature within \SI{8}{\minute} and is able to keep a steady-state margin of at least 
 \SI{+5.4}{\kelvin} above its dew point. Therefore can be concluded that the final trace pattern configuration is the recommended outcome of this study.

\subsection{Effect of Trace Material Conductivity}
\label{sec:material-discussion}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

A key finding of this study is the strong dependence of defroster performance on the trace electrical conductivity. The Joule heating power per unit length of trace scales as:
\begin{equation}
    P \propto \frac{V_\text{in}^2 \cdot \sigma_\text{eff}}{L_\text{trace}}
    \label{eq:power-scaling}
\end{equation}

where $\sigma_\text{eff}$ is the effective 2D conductivity from \Cref{eq:sigma-eff}. This explains why:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Materials with very high conductivity (Silver: $5.75 \times 10^7$, Gold: $4.2 \times 10^7$\,S/m) generate excessive heating, reaching temperatures of 415--465\,K at 20~traces --- far above safe operating limits.
    \item The optimal conductivity range for this geometry and voltage is $\sim 10^5$--$10^7$\,S/m, which corresponds to thick-film paste materials such as Pd--Ag alloy and Nichrome~V.
\end{itemize}

\Cref{fig:conductivity-effect} shows the simulated steady-state $T_\text{min}$ for all four EduPack materials at $n_\text{traces} = 20$, $V_\text{in} = \SI{12}{\volt}$, plotted against their electrical conductivity on a logarithmic scale. The two viable materials cluster just above the dew point line, while Silver and Gold overheat catastrophically.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.75\textwidth]{fig_conductivity_effect.png}
    \caption{Steady-state $T_\text{min}$ vs.\ trace electrical conductivity for all four EduPack materials ($n_\text{traces} = 20$, $V_\text{in} = \SI{12}{\volt}$). The green shaded region indicates the viable conductivity range. Silver and Gold fall far above the safe operating temperature.}
    \label{fig:conductivity-effect}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Trace Pattern Optimisation}
\label{sec:pattern-results}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

Two approaches to non-uniform trace spacing were investigated: a discrete zoned layout (Approach~A, Sim~4) and a continuous Chebyshev distribution (Approach~B, Sim~5). Both use 20~traces of Pd--Ag alloy at $V_\text{in} = \SI{12}{\volt}$ to allow direct comparison with the uniform baseline.

The zoned configuration places 6~traces in the top \SI{200}{\milli\metre}, 8~traces in the middle \SI{400}{\milli\metre}, and 6~traces in the bottom \SI{200}{\milli\metre}. This reduces the edge gap from \SI{38.1}{\milli\metre} (uniform) to \SI{28.6}{\milli\metre} while widening the centre gap to \SI{44.4}{\milli\metre}.

The time-dependent simulation shows that this configuration defrosts in \SI{11}{\minute} (vs.\ \SI{12}{\minute} for uniform) and reaches a steady-state $T_\text{min}$ of \SI{289.3}{\kelvin} (vs.\ \SI{288.2}{\kelvin}). The improvement is modest: \SI{1}{\minute} faster defrost and \SI{1.1}{\kelvin} higher $T_\text{min}$.

\subsubsection{Strengths}


\textcolor{red}{this whole chapter seems unnecessary}

The optimized trace pattern is able to achieve improved perfomance solely base on trace placement, at zero additional cost. 
The thermal stresses stay within safe limits (safety factor $\sim 3.1 \times$) and the design operates at nominal \SI{12}{\volt} 
battery voltage, meaning it works even with the engine off. The parametric approach (sweeping material, trace 
count, voltage, and spacing exponent) provides a comprehensive design map that allows easy trade-off analysis 
for different requirements, while the multiphysics coupling between Electric Currents, Heat Transfer, and Solid 
Mechanics captures the essential physics of the defroster operation. The mesh convergence study confirms that 
results are mesh-independent for the recommended configurations, and the boundary conditions ($h_\text{ext}$, 
$h_\text{int}$) are supported by published experimental data \cite{hozejowska2019}. \textbf{The design is also 
robust across real-world weather conditions (Sim~6)}: all five scenarios --- spanning ambient temperatures from 
\SIrange{-20}{10}{\celsius}, external convection coefficients from \SIrange{10}{40}{\watt\per\metre\squared\per\kelvin}, 
and physically realistic dew points --- were defrosted within \SI{8}{\minute}, with a minimum steady-state margin 
of \SI{5.4}{\kelvin} above the dew point (freezing rain scenario). Finally, the sequential solve strategy for the 
stress analysis overcomes the COMSOL~6.2 spatial frame limitation while still capturing the thermal stress state.

















\subsubsection{Limitations, Future Work, and Broader Implications}

The von Mises stress distribution for the optimised $p = 1.3$ design is shown in \Cref{fig:sim5-von-mises}.
The sharp 90\textdegree{} trace corners act as mathematical stress singularities, so the \emph{Surface Maximum}
($\sigma_\text{max} = \SI{2.7281e8}{\pascal}$) is mesh-dependent and not physically meaningful. The average
von Mises stress, $\sigma_\text{avg} = \SI{5.3651e6}{\pascal}$, lies well below the \SI{165}{\mega\pascal} limit
and confirms that the design is structurally safe.




\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{2D approximation:} The model uses a flat 2D geometry. A real rear window is curved, which affects heat flux distribution and stress concentrations. A 3D simulation with curved glass would be more realistic.

    \item \textbf{Fixed boundary conditions:} The fixed constraint at the glass edges overestimates the peak stress. Modelling the rubber seal as an elastic support would give more realistic stress values.

    \item \textbf{Constant heat transfer coefficients:} In reality, $h_\text{ext}$ depends on vehicle speed (forced convection) and $h_\text{int}$ depends on cabin HVAC settings. Sim~6 addressed the former by sweeping $h_\text{ext}$ from \SIrange{10}{40}{\watt\per\metre\squared\per\kelvin} across five weather scenarios; the effect of varying $h_\text{int}$ with HVAC state remains as future work.

    \item \textbf{Material property validation:} The EduPack values for Pd--Ag alloy are for bulk material. Screen-printed thick-film pastes may have different effective properties. Experimental validation with a physical prototype is recommended.

    \item \textbf{Contact resistance:} The model does not account for electrical contact resistance between traces and bus bars, which may reduce the effective heating power.
\end{itemize}

The Chebyshev spacing result has broader implications beyond this specific defroster. The key insight is that \textbf{non-uniform spatial distribution of heat sources}, optimised using a single continuous parameter, can be as effective as changing materials or operating conditions. This principle applies to any distributed heating or cooling problem where temperature uniformity is the objective.

In the context of automotive defrosters, the Chebyshev approach could be directly applied to front windshield defrosters (where driver visibility constraints are even more stringent) or to heated mirrors. The $p$-parameter provides a simple manufacturing specification: a single number that fully defines the trace layout for any given window height and trace count.

Furthermore, the observation that bulk-conductivity materials (Silver, Gold) overheat catastrophically in this geometry has a practical consequence: screen-printed thick-film pastes are not merely a cost-saving alternative to bulk metals --- they are \emph{functionally necessary} for the correct thermal behaviour. This finding is consistent with the industrial practice of using Pd--Ag and Ag paste formulations with controlled conductivity \cite{prudenziati2012}.
